Get started

Install a package and make your first quantum encoding in a few lines.

pip install quantumaudio
import quantumaudio as qa

scheme  = qa.load_scheme("qpam")   # pick an encoding scheme
circuit = scheme.encode(audio)     # audio  -> quantum circuit
audio2  = scheme.decode(circuit)   # circuit -> audio
Illustrative snippet — see the docs for the current API

Research

Shaping the mechanics of the next media era. Through scientific rigor, exploration and community-building, we’re uncovering how quantum will define it.

Media is shaped by the technological substrate of its time — from the printing press and photography to broadcast, digital and social media — each new technology giving rise to new mechanics for how content is created, distributed and experienced. Today’s systems, built on recommendation algorithms and generative AI, are straining under mass-disinformation, homogenised content and eroding trust. The next shift won’t come from refining them, but from a fundamentally new paradigm: quantum computing.

Quantum computing processes information in a radically different way — leveraging phenomena like superposition and entanglement rather than stepping through binary logic. That opens a unique set of affordances and a new kind of media mechanics, which we at MOTH are uncovering every day, and which will come to define how the next era of media is created, connected and experienced.

People

The team bending quantum towards culture. Spread across our offices in London, Basel and Brooklyn.

Moth began in 2022, when Eduardo Miranda’s Quantum Computer Music drew together two communities that rarely met — artists curious about quantum computing, and quantum researchers curious about art. Out of that convergence, and a quantum music project linking co-founder Harry Kumar with Quantinuum’s Ilyas Khan, the company set out to build the first consumer wave of quantum-powered creative tools.
